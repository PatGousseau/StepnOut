The line

This guide only includes public spaces, venues, and properly supervised climbing. Do not enter roofs, construction sites, rail corridors, closed parks, utility spaces, or anywhere posted as private; do not climb structures or cross barriers. Those spots can turn a fun night into a serious fall, arrest, or rescue situation very quickly.

Climb Base5 — actual vertical adventure

#2–1485 Welch St., North Vancouver

Their North Vancouver gym is open Friday 10 AM–10 PM. New climbers can use bouldering and auto-belays with no prior experience, partner, booking, or gear—just complete the waiver and get their orientation. Much more satisfying than trying to climb a building.

False Creek Olympic Village loop

Start / finish: Creekside Park gazebo

A 2.9 km, mostly flat waterfront loop that passes Science World, BC Place, Rogers Arena, Cooper’s Park, bridges, art, and Olympic Village. It’s built as a pedestrian-and-cycle-friendly seawall route—excellent for city lights and architecture.

Walk the city like it’s a set

These are ordinary public routes, not secluded back-route suggestions. Stay on lit, open paths; ride-share or transit back rather than cutting through unfamiliar industrial blocks late at night.

2.4 km · waterfront

False Creek North

Cooper’s Park → Yaletown / David Lam Park

The north shore of False Creek is a paved, relatively flat urban waterfront with marinas, bridges, glass towers, and the Roundhouse area. It is an easy way to keep the exploration feeling social and visible.

Stanley Park Seawall

Convention Centre → Stanley Park → English Bay

The seawall is Vancouver’s iconic continuous waterfront path. The Stanley Park portion takes two to three hours on foot, so use it at daylight or early dusk—not as a late-night shortcut through the park.

VPL rooftop garden + terraces

Central Library · 350 W Georgia St. · Levels 8–9

This is an actual public rooftop garden, not a “maybe it’s unlocked” roof. The library explicitly welcomes photography and provides Level 8 terraces and a Level 9 rooftop garden. It closes with the library at 6 PM on Fridays, so save it for another day.
